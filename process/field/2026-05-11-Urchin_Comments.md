# Urchin — Harris Dickinson, 2025

A movie that follows a young British man throughout his life between the streets of London and the effort of getting out of them, in a loop of self-pity, rage, confusion and lack of control.

## Interview with Director and Actor

He says he is fascinated to explore the subject of battling with addiction, and falling in the same spirals. He was going to prison, and did a lot of research with various associations. He doesn't want to be a saviour. He just cared about the topic.

## Interesting Scenes from Film / Observations

- Homeless vs homeless, or vs people who give some, but he uses violence to get more.
- Humour and exuberance, lack of control and lack of caring about others. Glimpses of freedom are a breath of fresh air, very beautiful.
- He carries a little cactus to the social worker.
- Vulnerability. People on the streets are vulnerable, and are looking for help. There is nothing to hide about it.
- He convinces himself that it is fine, he is not falling in the loop again.
- Fetal position while lying down.
- He looks at other homeless people playing music on the street with a caring glance.
- There is not much hope, not even in the last scene.
- Dreamy scenes as if they were a "safe space".
- Important: there are scenes that are happier, and it is NOT when people help him, but just when they chat, dance, eat together.
- Not always in the streets, it is about getting in and out of the hostels, of someone's van, of the bed of the night…
- The protagonist conceals his own understanding of his situation. He is sabotaging himself in a way. He does not seem aware of the consequences of his actions, nor is he able to take responsibility for them.
